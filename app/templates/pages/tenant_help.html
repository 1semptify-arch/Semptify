❓ Common Questions

After being served with an eviction summons and complaint, you typically have 7 days to file a written answer with the court. If you don't respond, the court may issue a default judgment against you. Do not ignore a summons. Contact Legal Aid (1-888-354-5522) or HOME Line (1-800-745-6686) immediately.
Minnesota law (MN Stat. 504B.161) requires landlords to maintain habitable conditions. Your remedies:

Rent Escrow — pay rent to the court until repairs are made.
Repair and Deduct — under certain conditions, deduct repair costs from rent.
Habitability Defense — use poor conditions as a defense in an eviction case.

Document everything first. Make repair requests in writing. Log every request in your journal with a date.
Yes. A counterclaim lets you sue in the same eviction case. Common grounds: habitability violations, illegal lockout, retaliation, security deposit violations, discrimination. A successful counterclaim can reduce or eliminate what you owe — and may result in damages to you.
Bring everything organized chronologically:

• Your written answer to the complaint
• Signed lease agreement
• All rent receipts
• Written repair requests and landlord responses
• Dated photos of the property
• All communications (texts, emails, notes)
• Your Semptify journal — your chronological record
• Government-issued ID
Gold standard: written request + dated photos + proof of delivery.

If the landlord doesn't respond in a reasonable time, follow up in writing and document that too.
In Minnesota, landlords must provide reasonable notice (generally 24 hours) before entering, except in emergencies. Repeated unauthorized entries may constitute harassment. Document every entry in your journal with date, time, and what occurred.
