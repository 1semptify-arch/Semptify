✉️ Template Letters

Professional letters you can edit and send to your landlord. Every letter creates a paper trail that protects you.

⚖️ Not Legal Advice — These templates are starting points. Adapt them to your exact situation. For complex or high-stakes situations, contact a licensed attorney or free legal aid. Full disclaimer →

🔧 Maintenance Request

Written repair request to your landlord

Use this to formally request repairs. Minnesota law (MN Stat. 504B.161) requires landlords to keep rental units habitable. A written request creates a legal record.

✅ Copied to clipboard

💰 Security Deposit Return Demand

Demand return of your security deposit

Minnesota law (MN Stat. 504B.178) requires landlords to return security deposits within 21 days after you move out, with an itemized statement. This letter demands compliance.

✅ Copied to clipboard

🏚️ Habitability Complaint

Formal complaint about unsafe or unlivable conditions

Use when conditions are severe and the landlord has not acted on your repair requests. This letter escalates the matter and formally invokes your rights under MN Stat. 504B.

✅ Copied to clipboard

📋 Response to Non-Renewal Notice

Formal response when landlord declines to renew

If you believe the non-renewal is retaliatory (e.g. after a repair complaint), document that clearly in this response. Under MN Stat. 504B.285, retaliation is illegal.
