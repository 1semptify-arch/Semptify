❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I have to respond to an eviction notice in Minnesota? ▼
After being served with an eviction summons and complaint, you typically have 7 days to file a written answer with the court. If you don't respond, the court may issue a default judgment against you. Do not ignore a summons. Contact Legal Aid (1-888-354-5522) or HOME Line (1-800-745-6686) immediately.
Can I be evicted in winter in Minnesota? ▼
Yes. The law does not have a general winter eviction moratorium. Eviction proceedings continue year-round. However, sheriffs have discretion about executing physical removal when temperatures are dangerously cold and no shelter is available — but this is discretionary, not a legal right. Always attend your court hearing regardless of season.
What if my landlord won't make repairs? ▼
Minnesota law (MN Stat. 504B.161) requires landlords to maintain habitable conditions. Your remedies include:

1. Rent Escrow — pay rent to the court instead of the landlord until repairs are made.
2. Repair and Deduct — under certain conditions, deduct repair costs from rent.
3. Habitability Defense — use poor conditions as a defense in an eviction case.

Document everything first. Use your Semptify journal to log every request with dates. Upload photos to your vault. Always make repair requests in writing.
Can I sue my landlord back? ▼
Yes. A counterclaim lets you sue your landlord in the same eviction case. Common counterclaims include:

• Habitability violations (unsafe conditions, failed repairs)
• Illegal lockout or utility shutoff
• Retaliation for exercising your rights
• Security deposit violations
• Discrimination

A successful counterclaim can reduce or eliminate what you owe, and may result in damages awarded to you.
Do I need a lawyer? ▼
You can represent yourself (called "pro se"). Semptify is built to help you do exactly that — organize your facts, understand the law, and document everything clearly.

That said, a lawyer significantly improves your chances in court. Free legal help is available:
• Legal Aid: 1-888-354-5522
• HOME Line: 1-800-745-6686
• LawHelpMN: lawhelpmn.org
What should I bring to court? ▼
Essential items:

• Your written answer/response to the complaint
• Signed lease agreement
• All rent receipts (every payment you've made)
• Written repair requests and landlord responses
• Photos of the property (dated)
• All communications with your landlord (texts, emails, notes)
• Your Semptify journal timeline — chronological record of events
• Government-issued ID

Organize documents chronologically. Judges need to see what happened and when.
How do I document a maintenance problem properly? ▼
The gold standard: written request + dated photos + proof of delivery.

1. Write the request (email or letter) — date it, describe the problem specifically
2. Take photos the same day — they embed a timestamp
3. Send via email (creates a record) or certified mail
4. Log it in your Semptify journal immediately
5. Upload the request and photos to your vault

If the landlord doesn't respond in a reasonable time, follow up in writing and document that too.
