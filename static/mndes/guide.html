How to Submit Exhibits to MNDES

Minnesota Digital Exhibit System — Required for all MN district court cases

Order ADM09-8010  |  Effective January 1, 2025
Semptify is NOT the court. Saving files in Semptify does not submit them to the court. You must complete your submission at the MNDES portal: mndigitalexhibitsystem.courts.state.mn.us
Court will NOT return your digital exhibits. Keep your own copies of everything you upload to MNDES.

Submission Steps

  1. 1
    Check your files in Semptify

    Use the MNDES compliance checker to confirm each file is on the Acceptable File Types List before uploading to the court.

  2. 2
    Convert non-compliant files

    Convert proprietary formats to MP4, MP3, PDF, or JPG. If conversion is impossible, contact the presiding judge BEFORE the hearing to request an exception (Order §6).

  3. 3
    Create or log in to MNDES

    Register at mndigitalexhibitsystem.courts.state.mn.us using your legal name and email. Works in Chrome, Edge, and Safari.

  4. 4
    Find your case

    Enter your MN case number (e.g., 19WS-CV-24-1234). Dashes are optional.

  5. 5
    Upload each exhibit individually

    Do NOT combine. Do NOT zip or compress. One file = one exhibit. Give each a descriptive name (e.g., "Photo of rear door — June 12").

  6. 6
    Set exhibit visibility

    Upload as "public" unless it's a medical record in a Civil Commitment case or a court order restricts access. Pre-hearing exhibits are automatically non-public.

  7. 7
    Share with the opposing party (if required)

    Use MNDES Share. If a no-contact order (OFP/HRO/DANCO) is active, do NOT use MNDES sharing — contact court administration for instructions first.

  8. 8
    Record your MNDES tracking numbers in Semptify

    Each uploaded file gets a unique tracking number. Enter it back into Semptify to complete your audit trail.

  9. 9
    At the hearing: OFFER each exhibit to the judge

    Uploading does NOT automatically make a file evidence. You must ask the judge to admit each exhibit during the hearing.

  10. 10
    Keep your own copies

    The court will not return digital exhibits. Retain originals on your device or cloud storage.

Accepted File Types

Source: MNDES Acceptable File Formats — mncourts.gov/mndes (updated Jan 2025)

Documents

PDF DOC DOCX RTF TXT XLS XLSX PPT PPTX

Images

JPG/JPEG PNG GIF TIFF/TIF BMP

Audio (jury-room eligible)

MP3 WAV M4A WMA AAC AIFF FLAC

Video (jury-room eligible)

MP4 MOV AVI WMV MKV FLV MPEG MPG M4V

What Cannot Go Into MNDES

  • Zipped or compressed archives (.zip, .rar, .7z, .tar, etc.)
  • Sexual content, nudity, or internet links to such content — submit as physical exhibit
  • Discovery documents (Alford Packets) — unless judge specifically orders it
  • Motion or affidavit attachments — use the eFile & eServe (eFS) system instead
  • Sealed case exhibits — call court admin at 651-377-7180 to upload on your behalf
  • In-camera review exhibits — deliver directly to judge's chambers
  • Certified copies authenticating originals — must be physical exhibits
  • Executable files, scripts, or any file type not on the Acceptable File Types List
  • Files larger than 100 GB — submit as physical exhibit
