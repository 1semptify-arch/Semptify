What is MNDES?

The Minnesota Digital Exhibit System (MNDES) is the mandatory web-based platform for submitting, managing, and accessing digital exhibits in all Minnesota district courts, effective January 1, 2025 under Supreme Court Order ADM09-8010.

  • ⚖️
    Mandatory statewide. All digital exhibits must be submitted via MNDES unless the presiding judge permits an exception for good cause.
  • 🔒
    Pre-hearing exhibits are NOT public records until offered in a public proceeding (Order §9). Upload does not automatically make a file evidence.
  • 🎧
    Audio & video admitted exhibits must be permitted in the jury room in criminal cases; civil cases are at the judge's discretion (Order §2/§3).
  • 📋
    File types are strictly controlled. Only the Acceptable File Types List is permitted. The State Court Administrator maintains and publishes the current list (Order §5).
  • 🚫
    No zipped/combined files. Each exhibit must be uploaded as a separate file. No archives.
  • 🗑️
    Courts can delete exhibits per records retention schedules without notifying the submitting party (Order §7). Keep your own copies.

Your Role-Specific Requirements

Tenant / Self-Represented Party

You are presenting your own case. This guide walks you through what you need to do step by step.

What You Must Do

  • 1
    Check your files before the hearing

    Use Semptify's MNDES compliance checker. Every file must be on the Acceptable File Types List. The checker flags problems automatically.

  • 2
    Convert any flagged files

    Use free tools: VLC or HandBrake for video, Audacity for audio, print-to-PDF for documents. If you can't convert, ask the judge for an exception at least a few days before the hearing.

  • 3
    Create an MNDES account

    Go to mndigitalexhibitsystem.courts.state.mn.us. Register with your legal name. Works in Chrome, Edge, Safari.

  • 4
    Upload each exhibit separately

    Find your case number. Click "New Submission." Upload ONE file at a time — do not zip or combine. Name it clearly (e.g., "Lease Agreement — March 2023").

  • 5
    Share with the other party (if required)

    Use MNDES Share. If you have a no-contact/protection order (OFP, HRO, DANCO): do NOT use MNDES sharing. Call court administration first.

  • 6
    Record your tracking numbers in Semptify

    MNDES assigns a tracking number to each uploaded file. Enter these back in Semptify to complete your audit trail.

  • 7
    At the hearing: say "I offer Exhibit 1"

    Uploading is not enough. You must verbally offer each exhibit to the judge during the hearing. The judge decides whether to admit it.

Critical Reminders

  • 🚨
    The court will not return your digital exhibits after the case closes. Keep your original files.
  • 🚨
    Uploading to Semptify does not submit to the court. You must go to MNDES and upload yourself.
  • ⚠️
    Do not upload discovery documents (Alford Packets) into MNDES unless a judge orders it.
  • ⚠️
    If your case is sealed or involves in-camera evidence, call the court at 651-377-7180 before uploading anything.
  • ✅
    You can upload exhibits as early as the day you receive your hearing notice — earlier is better.

Advocate

You assist clients with document preparation and submission readiness. You do not file on their behalf but ensure their exhibits meet MNDES standards.

Your MNDES Responsibilities

  • ✅
    Run compliance checks on all client exhibit files using Semptify before any hearing. Flag and resolve non-compliant files early.
  • ✅
    Guide conversion of proprietary formats. Help clients understand free tools (VLC, HandBrake, print-to-PDF).
  • ✅
    Screen for prohibited content. Verify files do not contain sexual content or nudity before client uploads. Confirm it's not discovery material.
  • ✅
    Flag no-contact order cases immediately. If OFP/HRO/DANCO is active, the client must NOT use MNDES Share. Direct them to court administration.
  • ✅
    Educate on the distinction: uploading to MNDES ≠ offering into evidence. Client must verbally offer at the hearing.
  • ✅
    Confirm client retains copies. Remind them the court does not return digital exhibits.
  • 🚫
    Do not upload for clients unless they have formally authorized you and you are a registered MNDES user for that case.
  • 🚫
    Motion/affidavit attachments are not MNDES exhibits — those go through eFile & eServe (eFS).

Multi-Client Compliance Workflow

  • 1
    Create a case package in Semptify

    Use the MNDES Package Builder for each client case. Semptify validates all files at once and produces a checklist.

  • 2
    Review the compliance summary

    Address all red flags (non-compliant types, proprietary formats, size issues) before the client's filing deadline.

  • 3
    Prepare the client

    Walk through the pre-submission checklist together. Confirm all attestations: no sexual content, not discovery, not a motion attachment.

  • 4

  • 5

    After upload, record the MNDES tracking numbers in Semptify for the audit trail.

Legal / Attorney

You are counsel of record. You have full MNDES submission authority and evidentiary responsibility for every exhibit you offer.

Compliance Obligations (Order ADM09-8010)

  • ⚖️
    Mandatory MNDES use. All electronic exhibits for MN district court cases must be submitted via MNDES (Order §1). No exceptions without presiding judge approval.
  • ⚖️
    Acceptable File Types only. Counsel is responsible for ensuring exhibits comply with the State Court Administrator's list (Order §5).
  • ⚖️
    Proprietary format obligation. Make "reasonable efforts" to convert proprietary formats (Order §6). If conversion is impossible, obtain judge permission before the hearing and provide: the file, any proprietary player/codecs, and playback instructions.
  • ⚖️
    Jury room implications. Admitted audio/video exhibits in criminal cases must be permitted in the jury room under Minn. R. Crim. P. 26.03, subd. 20 unless the trial judge excludes them (Order §2). Tag audio/video exhibits accordingly.
  • ⚖️
    Public access. Admitted exhibits are case records. Pre-hearing exhibits are not publicly accessible (Order §9). Opposing counsel access may be provided by email/transmission with judge approval (Order §8).
  • ⚖️
    Retention responsibility. Digital exhibits are presumed copies. Parties are presumed to retain them. The court may destroy MNDES exhibits per records schedules without notice (Order §7).
  • 🚫
    Sexual content / nudity prohibited in MNDES absolutely (Order §10). Must be submitted as physical exhibits.

Role-Specific Exhibit Workflow

  • 1
    Build exhibit list in Semptify

    Create an MNDES package. Tag each exhibit with its exhibit number per any scheduling order. Flag jury-room eligibility for A/V.

  • 2
    Validate file types

    Confirm all files pass the MNDES compliance check. Convert or seek exception for any non-conforming format.

  • 3
    Pre-file exchange (if required)

    Exchange exhibit list with opposing counsel per the court's scheduling order. Use MNDES Share unless a protection order applies.

  • 4
    Upload and confirm tracking numbers

    Log in to MNDES, upload each exhibit individually, record tracking numbers back in Semptify for the record.

  • 5
    At trial: offer and lay foundation

    Offer each exhibit. Lay proper evidentiary foundation. The judge's admission decision determines case record status.

Case Manager

You manage exhibit preparation and compliance across multiple client cases. You ensure no case goes to hearing with non-compliant files.

Management Responsibilities

  • 📋
    Triage all case files using MNDES compliance check before scheduling any hearing preparation session.
  • 📋
    Track judge exception requests. If any case has a proprietary format that cannot be converted, log the exception request, the judge's response, and the outcome in Semptify.
  • 📋
    Flag protection order cases immediately. Cases with OFP/HRO/DANCO require special handling for exhibit sharing. Never use MNDES Share without confirming there is no active protection order.
  • 📋
    Verify discovery separation. Confirm no discovery material (Alford Packets, interrogatory responses) is routed into the MNDES exhibit pipeline. Those belong in separate channels.
  • 📋
    Maintain audit trail. Record MNDES tracking numbers for each exhibit in Semptify as soon as uploads are confirmed.
  • 📋
    Remind clients to retain originals. Courts delete digital exhibits on records schedules without notice. Clients must keep copies.

Admin / Court-Facing Staff

This role includes Semptify administrators and court-adjacent staff supporting users. This section covers your responsibilities under Order ADM09-8010 and how Semptify enforces compliance on your side.

System-Level Compliance Points

  • 🔧
    File type enforcement is automatic. Semptify's mndes_compliance.py enforces the Acceptable File Types List at upload time. Blocked types return an error before the file is stored.
  • 🔧
    Archive/zip blocking is active. ZIP, RAR, 7Z, TAR, GZ and related archives are added to the blocked extension list in file_validator.py.
  • 🔧
    Audit trail. Every MNDES validation result, package creation, and attestation is logged via Semptify's audit logger. No MNDES action is silent.
  • 🔧
    No-contact order gates. When a case is flagged with OFP/HRO/DANCO, Semptify surfaces the warning and blocks exhibit sharing steps until acknowledged.
  • 🔧
    Sealed case block. Any package creation request for a sealed case returns a 422 with instructions to contact court administration at 651-377-7180.
  • 🔧
    File types version. The current list version is 2025-01. When the State Court Administrator updates the official list, update MNDES_ACCEPTABLE_TYPES in app/core/mndes_compliance.py and bump MNDES_FILE_TYPES_VERSION.
  • 🔧
    No public API to MNDES. Semptify cannot submit directly to MNDES. ManualPortalClient in mndes_api_client.py returns portal instructions. Swap to MNDESRestClient when MN courts release an API.
  • 🔧
    Navigation SSOT. All MNDES paths (/mndes/guide, /api/mndes/*) are registered in NavigationRegistry.COURT_FLOW. Never hardcode these paths in templates or routers.

Keeping Compliance Current

  • 1
    Monitor mncourts.gov/mndes

    The State Court Administrator periodically reviews and updates the Acceptable File Types List. Check when a new order is issued.

  • 2
    Update mndes_compliance.py

    Edit MNDES_ACCEPTABLE_TYPES, MNDES_PROHIBITED_TYPES, and MNDES_FILE_TYPES_VERSION. No other files need changes for file type updates.

  • 3
    Run compliance tests

    Run pytest tests/test_mndes_compliance.py after any update to the acceptable types list.

  • 4
    Update this guide

    Keep this compliance-guide.html synchronized with any rule changes from new court orders.
